<p><p>The roughness and contour measuring instruments market is divided into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact instruments utilize physical probes that touch the surface to measure roughness and contour, providing high accuracy but potentially altering the surface. Non-contact instruments, on the other hand, employ methods such as laser or optical scanning, allowing for measurement without surface interaction, which preserves the integrity of the material. Both types cater to various industries requiring precise surface analysis.</p></p>
