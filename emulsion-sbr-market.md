<p><p>The Emulsion Styrene-Butadiene Rubber (SBR) market comprises two primary types: Cold E-SBR and Hot E-SBR. Cold E-SBR is produced through a low-temperature polymerization process, resulting in a rubber with excellent elasticity and wear resistance, making it ideal for tire manufacturing. In contrast, Hot E-SBR is synthesized at higher temperatures, offering improved processing characteristics and stronger mechanical properties, which are suitable for various applications, including automotive and industrial products. Both types serve distinct market needs in the rubber industry.</p></p>
