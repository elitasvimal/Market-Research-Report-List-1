<p><p>The inverter heat pump market encompasses two main types: ducted and ductless systems. Ducted inverter heat pumps utilize a network of ducts to distribute heated or cooled air throughout a building, offering efficient temperature control for larger spaces. In contrast, ductless inverter heat pumps, or mini-splits, provide targeted heating and cooling through individual units mounted in specific rooms, enhancing energy efficiency and flexibility. Both types leverage inverter technology to adjust compressor speed, optimizing energy consumption and comfort.</p></p>
