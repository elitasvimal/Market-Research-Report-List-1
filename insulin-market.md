<p><p>The insulin market comprises three main types: Animal Insulin, Regular Human Insulin, and Insulin Analogs. Animal Insulin, derived from pig or cattle pancreases, was historically used but has largely been replaced. Regular Human Insulin, synthesized to mimic naturally occurring insulin, serves as a standard treatment method. Insulin Analogs, genetically engineered, offer improved pharmacokinetics, allowing for better blood sugar control with varying onset and duration profiles. Together, these options provide tailored diabetes management strategies for patients.</p></p>
