<p><p>The high-end beer market encompasses three main types: Ale, Lager, and Hybrid beers. Ales are characterized by their fruity and complex flavors, often brewed with top-fermenting yeast at warmer temperatures. Lagers are known for their crisp and clean profile, using bottom-fermenting yeast and cooler fermentation, resulting in a smoother taste. Hybrid beers combine elements of both ales and lagers, offering unique flavor profiles through varied yeast and brewing techniques, appealing to craft beer enthusiasts seeking diverse and innovative options.</p></p>
