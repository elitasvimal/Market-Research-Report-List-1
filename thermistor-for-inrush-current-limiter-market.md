<p><p>Thermistors for inrush current limiters are temperature-sensitive resistors used to manage electrical surges. Negative Temperature Coefficient (NTC) thermistors decrease resistance with rising temperature, effectively limiting initial current during power-up and quickly stabilizing. In contrast, Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) thermistors increase resistance with temperature, reducing current after an inrush event. Both types serve essential roles in protecting circuits from damage caused by excessive current, with applications ranging from consumer electronics to industrial equipment.</p></p>
