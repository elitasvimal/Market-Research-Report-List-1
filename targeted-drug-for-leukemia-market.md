<p><p>The targeted drug market for leukemia primarily includes two types: monoclonal antibodies and small-molecule drugs. Monoclonal antibodies are lab-engineered proteins designed to bind specific antigens on cancer cells, triggering an immune response to eliminate these cells. In contrast, small-molecule drugs are low molecular weight compounds that penetrate cells to inhibit specific enzymes or pathways critical for cancer cell proliferation. Together, these therapies enhance treatment efficacy and provide personalized options for leukemia management, catering to different patient needs and tumor characteristics.</p></p>
