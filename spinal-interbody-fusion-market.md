<p><p>Spinal interbody fusion is a surgical procedure aimed at stabilizing the spine by fusing adjacent vertebrae using implants inserted between them. This technique is commonly employed to treat conditions such as degenerative disc disease, spinal instability, and herniated discs. The procedure involves the removal of the intervertebral disc and the placement of a bone graft or implant to promote fusion, providing both structural support and pain relief.</p><p>The spinal interbody fusion market is experiencing significant growth, driven by an increase in spinal disorders, a rising aging population, and advancements in surgical techniques and technologies.
