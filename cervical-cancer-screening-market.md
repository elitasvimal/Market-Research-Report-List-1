<p><p>Cervical cancer screening encompasses several key methods: Pap tests, HPV tests, and Visual Inspection using Acetic Acid (VIA). Pap tests involve collecting cells from the cervix to identify abnormal changes. HPV tests specifically detect the presence of high-risk human papillomavirus strains that can lead to cervical cancer. Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid involves applying acetic acid to the cervix and visually assessing for abnormalities. These screening types aim to enhance early detection and improve outcomes for cervical cancer patients.</p></p>
