<p><p>The semi-automatic external defibrillator market includes two main types: non-synchronous defibrillators and synchronous defibrillators. Non-synchronous defibrillators deliver shocks without coordinating with the patient’s heart rhythm, typically used in emergencies to restore normal rhythm. In contrast, synchronous defibrillators are designed to synchronize shocks with the cardiac cycle, mainly employed for specific arrhythmias. Both types play critical roles in emergency medical situations, enabling timely intervention to improve survival rates during cardiac arrest incidents.</p></p>
