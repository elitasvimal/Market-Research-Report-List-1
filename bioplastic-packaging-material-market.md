<p><p>The bioplastic packaging material market is divided into two main types: biodegradable and non-biodegradable materials. Biodegradable materials break down naturally over time, reducing environmental impact and supporting waste management efforts. Common examples include polylactic acid (PLA) and polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA). In contrast, non-biodegradable materials offer durability and extended usage life, but may require special conditions to decompose and can contribute to plastic pollution. Both types serve varying industries, balancing sustainability with functional packaging solutions.</p></p>
